What are BÃ©zier curves?

BÃ©zier curves are used in many applications. They were first used to plot curves by Pierre BÃ©zier in 1962, using them to model automotive bodies. In design, these curves are often used to greate so-called vector graphics; images that can be scaled without loss in quality, as opposed by raster graphics where pixels become visible if the image is scaled up.

A second application can be found in your browser. Sure, many browsers are capable of creating and displaying SVG (Scalable Vector Graphics) images, but the curves are more basic and subtle than that. Almost every animation/transition utilizes a BÃ©zier curve for timing. You may want your animation to start slow, then accelerate, then slow down. You bet there's a BÃ©zier curve for that!
